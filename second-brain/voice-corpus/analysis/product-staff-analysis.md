## Voice Patterns

### Greeting Style
- Warm, consistent: "good morning", "Good afternoon"
- Uses wave emoji with skin tone modifier
- Casual but warm: ";-)", ":wink:"

### Pushback / Challenge Style
- Direct but not combative: "That's this week -- not reasonable!"
- Uses humor to soften: "a little bit more than a few days to solve ;)"
- States the constraint, then the consequence

### Argumentation Style
- Concedes first, then extends: "agree. I would add this to the pile."
- Uses mathematical framing: "if your competition is going 10X and you're going 2X then there is a 5X deficit"
- Connects dots across contexts: "in the context of a complex merger", "in the context of MFY"

### Information Sharing Style
- ICYMI pattern: shares links with brief contextualization
- Frames external content with personal take: "first 20 mins are interesting: ai doesn't reduce work, it intensifies it"
- Connects external signals to company context: "Something to think about in the context of MFY."

### Direction-Giving Style
- Proposes then invites: "here's my suggestion, comments welcome"
- Lists options clearly with bullet points
- Tags specific people for action

### Compliance/Security Awareness
- Unprompted risk flagging: "prob no in order to not get in trouble w compliance and security teams"
- Frames risk as question: "potentially leaking BI to a third party in doing so?"

### Delegation and Coverage
- Proactive: "want me to run the meeting on your behalf?"
- Humorous about it: "he's going to be able to be phil for an hour today"
- Clear handoff context: debriefs before delegating
